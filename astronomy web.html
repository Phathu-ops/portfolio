ğŸŒ’ Solar Eclipse

A solar eclipse occurs when the Moon passes between the Earth and the Sun, temporarily blocking the Sun's light. There are total, partial, and annular eclipses.

  • Next Total Solar Eclipse: August 12, 2026 (visible in parts of Europe, North Africa, and the Arctic)
  • Best Viewing: Use certified eclipse glasses. Find a location with a clear view of the sky, away from city lights.
  • Guide: Arrive early, check weather, and never look directly at the Sun without protection.

ğŸŒ  Meteor Showers

Meteor showers are events where many meteors are observed to radiate from one point in the night sky. They occur when Earth passes through debris left by comets.

  • Perseids: Peaks August 12-13, best viewed after midnight, look northeast.
  • Geminids: Peaks December 13-14, visible all night, look near the constellation Gemini.
  • Best Viewing: Find a dark spot away from city lights, bring a blanket, and allow your eyes to adjust for 20 minutes.
  • Guide: No equipment needed, just look up! Meteors can appear anywhere but radiate from the shower's "radiant" point.
